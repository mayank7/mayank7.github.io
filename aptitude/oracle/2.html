12) A charitable woman gave one penny more than half pennies in her purse to a farmer. Afterwards she came across a beggar whom she gave 2 pennies more than half of what she had in her purse.
In the end she gave 3 pennies more than half of what she had in her purse and left with a penny with her to a boy. Find what she had in the beginning?

Soln:
Let there b 'x'pennies
now to the farmer:
she gave 1 penny more than the half in the purse i.e (x/2 +1)
now she is left with:: (x-(x/2+1))=(x-2)/2
now to the beggar::
she gave 2 pennies more than the half in the purse i.e(x-2)/4 +2
and she is left with :: ((x-2)/2)-((x-2)/4 +2)= (x-10)/4
now at last
she gave 3 pennies more then the half she was left with i.e ((x-10)/8+ 3)=A
so she is left with
(x-10)/4-A=(x-34)/8
now acc. to quest
(x-34)/8=1
hence x=42.
